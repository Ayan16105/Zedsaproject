OUR
STORY

Born out of a labour of love and a dedication to creative diligence, HOGA Films came into being in Jaipur,2018, with 2021 marking a pivotal year in which we introduced our second office in Bombay. What kickstarted as a small media house with just four members, has since evolved into a dynamaic group of creative minds with a core collective vision.

With a foundation built with accredited individual achievements, our founders have worked with acclaimed filmmakers,international films festivals,a documentory feature in Incredible India, platforms such as Netflix, Amazon, BBC India, along with being invited as a speaker for esteemed brands such as TEDx, Google talks, Columbia and Qatar Foundation. Together, our goal is to create narratives through cross-cultural storytelling, and breaking boundaries with the universal language of creativity.

While documentary films and live events marked our earlier projects, we remained undeterred by the challenges of the COVID-19 pandemic, through which we launched a sister concern brand, Lockdown Shorts Studio. The latter was a culmination of films created with the global limitation of not being able to set foot outside our homes an achievement that brought fulfilment on both a human, and creatively humbling level. Along with the challenges of production during the pandemic,securing a platform to show-case our work posed an even bigger challenge. Post ten months of perseverance,the fruits of our labour transpired in the form of a contarct with Disney+Hotstar, our entry into the OTT World which received recognition from leading news agencies. Under the banner of Lockdown Shorts Studio, we have since produced the trending 'Ishq Express'(IMDb rating - 7.7/10) and 'Uljhey Hyue' (IMDb rating 8.1/10) for Amazon's Mini TV. The appreciation we garnered, encouraged us to establish a content bank, beginning with 'PROVIDER' a six-part we series.

Producer
Wasim Chishty

Wasim was born and brought up in the sound of Sufism and Qawwali. Starting his journey in Mumbai as a Model at various college fests, he acquired the title of Best Talent in Grasim Mr India. Wasim went on to work in various short films along with feature films, where his production journey began in the film "Udaan", working with acclaimed director, Vikramaditya Motwane. His later credits include his work as the line producer in the Ajmer schedule of Imtiaz Ali’s “Highway”. Wasim is an avid poetry enthusiast as well as a writer of poetry, influencing his love and style of creative writing, while also holding the strong skillset and core understanding of the world of production. Inspired by Rumi’s quote; 'Appear as you are, be as you appear,’ Wasim approaches both life and his creative work with the intention and practice of utmost honesty.

Producer
Manvendra Singh shekhawat

Manvendra, an explorer, creator, and entrepreneur, has brought ambitious ideas to life through his generational thinking, systems approach, creative mindset, and unwavering perseverance. Rooted in his family's humble beginnings, his entrepreneurial journey is fuelled by his unique perspectives that allow him to think globally while actively engaging at the grassroots level. His hotels, Suryagarh in Jaisalmer and Narendra Bhawan in Bikaner, stand as outliers, playing a pivotal role in putting these destinations on India's luxury tourism map. Currently, he is in the process of restoring five forts and palaces, envisioning their transformation into a network of cultural hubs housing multiple cultural enterprises. As a community leader, he has forged alliances with local communities, addressing systemic problems ... Read More through over 50 community projects. These initiatives range from Jaisalmer's largest cleanliness and conservation drive to launching India's inaugural citizen-airline partnership, reinforcing regional tourism ecosystems. Having been invited to share his valuable insights international platforms such as, INK, Google, Columbia University, Qatar Foundation, Desi Miami, and UNWTO, Manvendra's current focus is on innovating the future of liveability. His sandbox project, Dhun, is a regenerative habitat in the making for 8,500 people. Over the past 8 years, he and his team have transformed 500 acres of barren land into a thriving ecosphere. Today, Manvendra is in the process of establishing an ecosystem of creative enterprises that leverage culture, nature, creativity, and technology, to craft developmental narratives, that foster self-sufficiency and equal opportunities in the developing world. Our proudest and longest project at HOGA Films is documenting the journey of Dhun ; the 500-acre suburban neighbourhood of Jaipur that redefines living development practices to provide freedom, inspiration, and resources. Today, we are thrilled to be handling the project’s complete communication. HOGA Films took the backstage, choreographing the marketing and communication campaign for the Learning Summit in Jaisalmer, brought together by Roundglass in February 2020. A campaign in which 120 leaders in Education from around the globe came together to develop a framework for learning that promotes universal well-being. In 2021, driven by a collective need for recovery post the COVID-19 pandemic, HOGA Films had the privilege to run the citizen airline marketing campaign, and are proud to have been a part of the inspiring initiative that became a lifeline for Jaisalmer's tourism industry, the "citizen-airline partnership model.” Not only did this bring back airlines to Jaisalmer, but also revived the tourism industry; a ground breaking campaign that has become a case study today.
